About this Internship

Jan 2021 - Sep 2021 (9 months)

Owing to the huge amount of textual information in digital form growing retrieving relevant information through information retrieval (IR) systems has become crucial. Due to the growing popularity of various tools such as voice assistants and chatbots, that rely on humanâ€“machine interaction, it is important that such systems are able to answer any query directly, rather than leading the user to a set of references. In this work, an end-to-end pipeline is proposed that performs this task by creating a Knowledge Graph from the corpus. It uses embeddings to predict the missing links in the knowledge graph and a unique answer selection module in order to reach the correct answer. The proposed methodology was tested on the SciQ and Aristo datasets and a comparative study was conducted to highlight the aspects on which information extraction models can improve.
